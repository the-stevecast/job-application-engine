## Example (for reference)

> **Core Positioning**
> I'm a product manager who turns ambiguous customer problems into shipped products — with a track record of doing it in regulated, high-stakes markets where the cost of getting it wrong is high.
>
> **What I Am**
> - A discovery-first PM who interviews users before writing requirements
> - A strong cross-functional partner — engineering and design trust me to translate between business and technical
> - Comfortable with compliance and regulatory constraints without using them as an excuse to slow down
> - Equally effective in 0-to-1 and scale-up product phases
>
> **What I'm Not**
> - Not a roadmap administrator who waits for stakeholders to tell me what to build
> - Not a PM who needs a large team to be effective — I've shipped with teams of 3 and teams of 30
>
> **Career Reframe**
> My stint at a startup that didn't make it isn't a gap — it's where I learned to make product decisions with incomplete data and limited runway. That experience made me a better PM than two years at a large company would have.
>
> **Proof Themes**
> 1. Identified a workflow gap through customer interviews → defined the problem and got it prioritized → shipped a feature that reduced customer support tickets by 40%
> 2. Inherited a stalled project with misaligned stakeholders → ran a structured alignment session → relaunched with a clear scope and shipped on time
> 3. Worked in a regulated environment (HIPAA) → learned the constraints → built a compliance review process that didn't bottleneck development
