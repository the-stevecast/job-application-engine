## Example (for reference)

> **Must-Haves**
> - Ownership of a product area or product line — not a supporting role
> - Collaborative engineering culture (not one where PM and Eng are adversarial)
> - Series B or later — I need some infrastructure to work with
>
> **Avoid**
> - Project manager or program manager roles disguised as PM roles
> - Roles where the PM is primarily a backlog manager rather than a strategic owner
> - Pure enterprise sales support roles (sales enablement, solutions consulting)
> - Companies where product strategy is entirely dictated by the sales team
>
> **Hard Constraints**
> - No relocation — remote or hybrid within 30 miles of [City]
> - No companies with fewer than 20 employees — I need some organizational support
>
> **Fit Notes**
> Platform PM roles are fine even though they're more technical than my background — I ramp quickly and the core PM skills transfer. What I want to avoid is roles where "technical PM" means "write tickets for engineers."
