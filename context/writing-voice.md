## Example (for reference)

> **Overall Tone**
> Direct and clear, with some warmth. I don't like formal corporate language, but I'm also not casual to the point of being unprofessional. Confident without being arrogant.
>
> **How I Explain Things**
> I lead with the concrete before the abstract. I'd rather say "we cut intake time from 14 minutes to 4" than "we significantly improved operational efficiency." I use short sentences. I don't bury the lead.
>
> **Style Preferences**
> - Plain language — if a simpler word works, use it
> - Active voice always
> - Short paragraphs (3-4 sentences max)
> - Specifics over generalities — concrete examples over sweeping claims
>
> **What to Avoid**
> - Em-dashes — I find them choppy
> - Buzzwords: "passionate about", "results-driven", "synergy", "leverage" (as a verb), "robust"
> - Sentences that start with "I am excited to..."
> - Anything that sounds like it came from a template
>
> **Cover Letter Tone**
> Peer-to-peer. I'm writing to a hiring manager as a professional speaking to another professional, not as an applicant asking for a chance. I state what I do, why it maps to what they need, and invite a conversation. Confident but not arrogant.
