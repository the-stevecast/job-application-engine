# Cover Letter Template

## Header Format

[Three visual groups separated by blank lines. Use `<br>` tags for line breaks within a group.]

```
[YOUR NAME]<br>
[City, State]<br>
[phone] | [email] | [linkedin.com/in/yourhandle](https://linkedin.com/in/yourhandle)

[Date]

[Company Name]<br>
[Role Title]

---
```

**Rendering notes:**
- `<br>` after the name forces it onto its own line (styled large/bold by CSS `::first-line`).
- `<br>` after location forces phone/email/linkedin to a third line.
- Date is its own paragraph (blank line above and below).
- Company and role are in one paragraph with `<br>` between them.
- The `---` horizontal rule separates the header from the body.

---

## Body Structure

[Tone is set by `context/writing-voice.md`. Follow it. Default: confident, peer-to-peer — stating what you do and why it maps to what they need. Not pitching, not flattering.]

### Paragraph 1: Lead with Capability
Open with what you bring, not praise of the company. State the core capability or experience that maps to this role. Connect it to the role briefly, but the emphasis is on what you've done. A company-specific hook can appear naturally but must not be the opening sentence.

### Paragraph 2: Operational Example
One concrete example showing the work: specific situation, what was built or designed, and the operational result. This paragraph proves the claim from paragraph 1. Keep it grounded and specific.

### Paragraph 3: Differentiator
What makes your approach distinctive — and how it connects to this role's specific needs. This is what separates you from other qualified candidates.

### Paragraph 4: Close
Direct, confident close. Express interest in a conversation. No desperation, no "I would be honored." State what you want to discuss and leave it there.

Best regards,
[Your Name]

---

## Rules

- 4 paragraphs max. Each paragraph 3-5 sentences.
- Tone: follow `context/writing-voice.md`. If no preference is set, default to confident and peer-to-peer.
- Never open with company praise ("I'm excited about...", "I was thrilled to see...", "Your company's mission...").
- Open with capability: what you do, how long you've done it, and what it means for this role.
- Must include one operational example with a specific before/after or system built.
- Must include a differentiator — not just what was built, but what makes your approach work.
- Follow `context/writing-voice.md` for tone, style, and avoid list.
- Company-specific references should be woven in naturally, not front-loaded as flattery.
